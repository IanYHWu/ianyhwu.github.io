## Research

My research centres on exploring and optimising the ability of LLMs to <span style="color: #1ABC9C;">**learn, reason and adapt at test time**</span>: how can we best *train* models to utilise lots and lots of test-time compute to solve very complex and very long-horizon reasoning tasks? Some specific sub-problems I am aiming to solve:
- **Memory**: for LLMs to learn over very long horizons, they must possess memory of their past actions and the consequences of these actions. Can we optimise the process by which LLMs write and read from their own memory and context? And how should we parameterise or otherwise represent this memory?
- **Exploration**: an LLM agent endowed with good memory systems should sometimes take actions that yield *the most useful memories* rather than the highest immediate rewards, especially on long-horizon tasks. Can we train LLMs that possess this exploration instinct? And how should we even measure the capacity for a model to explore?
- **Credit assignment**: for LLMs to learn from their own actions autonomously, they must be able to identify their own good actions and extract useful learning signal from them on the fly. Can we improve the ability of LLMs to do this on very difficult tasks, and possibly without relying on environment feedback?
